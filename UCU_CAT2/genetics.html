What is Genetics?

The branch of biology that studies the heredity and variation of inherited traits in living organisms. It involves the study of genes, DNA, chromosomes, and genetic variation, and how they are passed down from one generation to the next. Genetics helps us understand how traits are inherited from parents to offspring, and how the expression of those traits can be influenced by various factors, including the environment. It also plays a crucial role in many areas of biology and medicine, including the study of evolution, the diagnosis and treatment of genetic disorders, and the development of genetic engineering techniques.

DNA (Deoxyribonucleic Acid)

The genetic material that carries the hereditary information in all living organisms. Understanding the structure, replication, and function of DNA is crucial to understanding genetics

Chromosomes

Cell organelles are specialized structures within a cell that carry out specific functions necessary for the survival of the cell and the organism as a whole. Different types of cells have different organelles, and some organelles are only present in certain types of cells.

Genetic Disorders

conditions caused by mutations or abnormalities in genes or chromosomes. Understanding the causes, inheritance patterns, and treatments of genetic disorders is an important aspect of genetics.

Genetic Engineering

The deliberate modification of an organism's genetic material using biotechnology. The study of genetic engineering techniques and their applications is an important aspect of modern genetics.
